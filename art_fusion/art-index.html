ArtFusion: UI Design

Case Study | Sign-Up Page for an Art Showcasing Event

Duration: 4 days

Background

ArtFusion is an annual community-driven art showcasing event that brings together local artists, emerging art students, galleries, and art enthusiasts. The goal of this design challenge was to create a seamless and intuitive sign-up experience that allows artists to submit their work for consideration, while also encouraging community engagement and social sharing.

Project Overview

This project was inspired by a Daily UI prompt focused on designing a sign-up page. I took it a step further by conceptualizing a responsive 3-step sign-up flow tailored specifically for the art community.

The challenge was to balance usability and aesthetics—making the process inviting for artists of all backgrounds, while also ensuring the user experience was smooth across mobile, tablet, and desktop devices.

Design Goals

  1. Create an intuitive sign-up process tailored for artists.
  2. Collect all relevant information needed for event curation.
  3. Ensure a consistent and responsive experience across all devices.
  4. Encourage post-submission engagement and social sharing.

Target Audience

  • Demographics: Local artists, art students, and professional art organizations.
  • Age Range: 18–65
  • Device Usage: Mobile phones, tablets, and desktops.

User Flow

  1. Landing Page:
    • Quick introduction to ArtFusion
    • Hero image with clear CTA
    • Highlights of artist benefits
    • Footer with social links and contact info
  2. 3-Step Sign-Up Form:
    • Step 1: Artist Info
      • Name
      • Email
      • Phone Number
    • Step 2: Artwork Details
      • Title
      • Medium
      • Dimensions
      • Image Upload
    • Step 3: Submission Requirements
      • Artist Statement
      • Additional Notes
      • CTA to Submit
  3. Confirmation Page
    • Success message with submission summary
    • CTA to share the event on social media
    • Option to download a PDF confirmation

Design Decisions

  1. Minimalist Layout:

    Focus on clarity and ease of use. White space and clean forms reduce cognitive load.

  2. Progressive Disclosure:

    Dividing the form into steps keeps users engaged and prevents overwhelm.

  3. Responsive Design:

    All layouts were designed to adapt beautifully across screen sizes.

  4. Social Integration:

    Post-submission page includes a gentle nudge to share the event—helping boost reach and artist exposure.

Tools Used

  • Figma: UI design + prototyping
  • Illustrator: For icons and vector elements
  • Photoshop: Image prep

Reflection

This project gave me the opportunity to think critically about form design for a creative audience. Artists tend to value intuitive, visually engaging experiences, so the challenge was finding a balance between artistic flair and usability. I focused on simplicity, clarity, and responsiveness, which I believe made the design feel both professional and approachable.
